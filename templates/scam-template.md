# Phishing

## Summary
Phishing is a social engineering scam in which attackers impersonate legitimate organizations or trusted entities to trick victims into revealing sensitive information such as login credentials, financial data, or personal identification details. It is typically delivered via email, SMS, or social media and directs victims to fraudulent websites that mimic legitimate services. Phishing is one of the most widespread and scalable scam types globally and often serves as the entry point for larger fraud schemes and account takeovers.

---

## How It Works

1. **Initial Contact**  
   The victim receives a message appearing to originate from a trusted organization (e.g., bank, government agency, employer, technology provider).

2. **Trust Establishment / Urgency Trigger**  
   The message claims urgent action is required (account suspension, suspicious login, payment issue, security verification).

3. **Redirection to Fraudulent Infrastructure**  
   The victim is directed to a spoofed website or form that visually resembles the legitimate service.

4. **Data Harvesting**  
   The victim enters login credentials, payment details, or personal identification information.

5. **Exploitation Phase**  
   Stolen information is used for account takeover, financial fraud, identity theft, or sold on underground marketplaces.

---

## Psychological Tactics Used

- Authority  
- Urgency  
- Fear  
- Scarcity  
- Confusion  

---

## Technical Indicators (Detection Clues)

- Slightly misspelled or lookalike domains  
- Sender email addresses that do not match official domains  
- Links that redirect through multiple shortened URLs  
- Generic greetings instead of personalized communication  
- Poorly implemented HTTPS certificates or newly registered domains  
- Inconsistent branding, formatting, or grammar  

---

## Common Red Flags

- Requests for login credentials via email or SMS  
- Threats of immediate account suspension  
- Unexpected password reset notifications  
- Links that do not match the visible display text  
- Attachments requiring credential entry before viewing  

---

## Prevention Guidance

- Verify sender email domains carefully  
- Manually navigate to websites instead of clicking links  
- Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on accounts  
- Use email filtering and anti-phishing protections  
- Educate users on recognizing social engineering patterns  

---

## What To Do If Targeted

- Do not click links or download attachments  
- Report the message to the impersonated organization  
- Change passwords immediately if credentials were entered  
- Enable MFA if not already enabled  
- Monitor financial and online accounts for suspicious activity  
- File a report with appropriate national cybercrime authorities
